5 Apps That Can Protect Your Android From Hacking

03 October 2024 | Posts

Pearl Ramson

I once wrote a blog post about detecting and removing malware from PCs using Windows’ built-in utility applications. And, somehow, the aspect of using Windows built-in utility programs has sparked curiosity in some pals to find out effective android equivalents.

While there may be no android equivalent, some third-party applications can serve same purpose, as this post will show. Let’s dive in.

Why Android Security is Paramount

Device security has never been more critical than this era.

According to IOT analytics report, there are about 18.8 billion Internet of Things (IoT) devices currently in use globally, and this number is growing at an annual rate of approximately 13%. Projections estimate this figure will surpass 29 billion IoT devices by 2030. These include smartphones, tablets, and other connected devices in consumer, industrial, and enterprise settings

With the growing reliance on mobile devices for personal and professional activities comes the attendant consequence in the unprecedented increase in attacks and exploitations.

Generally, mobile phones are more susceptible to threats than PC because of their constant internet connection. Regular internet connectivity via Wi-Fi or cellular data means increased exposure to threats.

Attackers can also exploit android users through third-party stores, where they can lure users to install malicious apps unknowingly. Through the app, they can gain remote access to their device for whatever purpose.

Many phone users also have little awareness of cybersecurity risks, show a high level of negligence and low security-consciousness – and malicious actors have always exploit this to their advantage.

Even security-conscious individuals out there often have limited tools at their disposal to monitor and manage their phones’ security. So, it is less surprising the curiosity behind knowing applications that effectively manage android security.

Monitoring and Thwarting Attacks on Android Phones

Primary Protection First

Before we move on, this post only focuses on secondary security measures.

Primary preventive measures such as using strong password, updating OS and apps regularly, installing anti-virus app, avoiding suspicious links, downloading apps only from trusted sources, etc., must firstly be in place.

Third-Party Protection Apps

The focus from this point onward is reviewing third-party applications that are effective in monitoring and preventing android device’s breach.

For an attacker to gain remote access to your device, they need to create a backdoor entry through malware to establish a connection. This means they have to establish a connection between them and your device.

Whether as a proactive or reactive measures, these third-party applications are excellent Windows equivalent in monitoring and preventing such malicious connection in real time. They are capable of fishing out compromised application and take necessary actions as deem fit.

App Monitoring apps

PCAPdroid

To say having PCAPdroid installed on every smart phone is necessary is not an out-of-the-line advice. This app provides an all-in-one ultimate solution to android security issues.

It performs several security tasks from analyzing installed and deleted apps for vulnerabilities to monitoring apps’ data transfers to detect malware in the payload.

Asides the malware detection and no-root firewall features locked in premium, it is entirely free and available on Google playstore.

Inware

Inware is to android what Resource Monitor via Task manager is to PC – they both give you an overview of your device and real-time resource allocation. This information can enable you to monitor your device usage and detect anomalies asap.

Whether you need real-time info regarding your RAM usage, hardware clusters, fingerprints and bootloaders, etc., Inware can give you such insight.

Trustd

Trustd can help you defeat spyware, ransomware, and all other type of attacks. With its deep learning capability, it scans your device to fish out potentially malicious apps. It also alerts you whenever any app tries to gain access to your phone’s microphone or other essentials.

Formerly Traced, Trustd is entirely free and ads-free.

Network monitoring apps

NetGuard

NetGuard is a no-root firewall that monitors and controls all inbound and outbound network traffic on your device. It allows you to block or permit internet access on a per-app basis.

You can also view logs to detect unusual activity, such as apps attempting unauthorized internet connections, and block malicious apps from accessing sensitive data.

GlassWire

GlassWire provides an intuitive way to monitor your device's data usage and identify anomalies in network behavior.

Besides using detailed graphs to identify apps using excessive data unexpectedly, it also offers real-time data monitoring and historical usage analysis features.

With GlassWire, you can be sure to be on course to fight spyware and other behind the door attacks, as it alerts you for unusual activity and new app installations from time to time.

Android Security is Non-Negotiable

In this era, smartphones have become indispensable, so also their security is non-negotiable. Be vigilant. Always stay one-step ahead of malicious actors to keep your digital life secure with the right information.
